Understanding Gender Identity

An introduction to gender identity concepts and terminology.

By Dr. Jane Smith • May 15, 2023

Gender identity refers to a person's deeply felt internal and individual experience of gender, which may or may not correspond with the sex assigned to them at birth. Understanding gender identity is crucial for creating inclusive and affirming environments for everyone.

Key Concepts

Sex Assigned at Birth

Sex assigned at birth typically refers to the designation of a person as male, female, or intersex based on physical characteristics such as chromosomes, hormones, and anatomy. This assignment is usually made at birth and recorded on a birth certificate.

Gender Identity

Gender identity is one's internal sense of being male, female, both, neither, or somewhere along the gender spectrum. It's an internal experience that isn't necessarily visible to others.

Gender Expression

Gender expression refers to how a person presents their gender to the world through clothing, hairstyle, voice, behavior, and other external characteristics. Gender expression may or may not align with a person's gender identity.

Cisgender

Cisgender (often abbreviated as "cis") describes a person whose gender identity aligns with their sex assigned at birth. For example, someone assigned female at birth who identifies as a woman is cisgender.

Transgender

Transgender (often abbreviated as "trans") describes a person whose gender identity differs from their sex assigned at birth. For example, someone assigned male at birth who identifies as a woman is a transgender woman.

Non-binary

Non-binary is an umbrella term for gender identities that fall outside the binary categories of man or woman. Non-binary people may identify as having a gender between or beyond the spectrum of man and woman, having multiple genders, having no gender, or having a fluctuating gender identity.

The Spectrum of Gender

Gender is increasingly understood as a spectrum rather than a rigid binary of male and female. Many cultures throughout history have recognized gender identities beyond the binary, including:

  • Two-Spirit (Indigenous North American cultures)
  • Hijra (South Asian cultures)
  • Fa'afafine (Samoan culture)
  • Māhū (Native Hawaiian and Tahitian cultures)

Respecting Gender Identity

Respecting someone's gender identity is a fundamental aspect of treating them with dignity. This includes:

  • Using a person's correct name and pronouns
  • Not making assumptions about a person's gender based on their appearance
  • Recognizing that gender identity is personal and not subject to others' approval
  • Understanding that a person's gender identity may evolve over time

Conclusion

Understanding gender identity is an ongoing process as our collective knowledge evolves. Being open to learning and respecting how people identify is essential for creating a world where everyone can express their authentic selves.
